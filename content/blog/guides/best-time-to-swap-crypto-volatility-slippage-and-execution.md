There is no single “perfect” time to swap crypto.

But there are definitely bad timing windows where you are more likely to get poor execution.

## What timing actually changes

Timing affects:
- quote stability
- slippage risk
- execution speed
- probability of route degradation

In volatile windows, small delays can materially reduce final receive amount.

## Better timing principles

### 1) Avoid major news release spikes
During high-impact news, spreads widen and quotes move fast.

### 2) Prefer calmer liquidity windows for large swaps
Large tickets in thin books are more exposed to drift.

### 3) Watch your pair’s behavior, not the whole market
BTC may be calm while your route asset is moving aggressively.

### 4) Use protection settings intentionally
Very tight protection can increase rejects; very loose can increase loss.

### 5) Split size if needed
For larger swaps, splitting can reduce slippage impact.

## Signs you should wait a bit

- quote changes materially every refresh
- large difference between top providers
- repeated requote behavior
- unusual delay in order progression

## Signs timing is acceptable

- output stable across consecutive checks
- provider spread differences are small
- execution states progress normally

## Tactical workflow

1. Quote now
2. Refresh in 1–2 minutes
3. Compare stability
4. Execute when drift is acceptable for your target
