Swapping **Cardano (ADA)** on a DEX-style route can be efficient, but only if you understand the mechanics.

This guide explains what happens during an ADA swap and where users usually lose value.

## What happens during an ADA swap

Typical flow:

1. You request a quote for ADA pair conversion.
2. A route/provider is selected based on available liquidity.
3. You send ADA to the assigned deposit address.
4. The provider executes and sends output asset to destination address.

## What affects your final received amount

- liquidity depth for the pair
- route spread and fees
- market movement during execution
- destination chain/network costs

## Key risks in ADA DEX swaps

### Slippage and route drift
If market conditions move during execution, output can deviate from quote.

### Wrong destination/network details
Address/network mistakes remain one of the top causes of failed outcomes.

### Over-optimizing for headline quote
A tiny quote advantage may not survive execution if route reliability is weaker.

## How to improve ADA swap outcomes

- Compare route options before sending
- Validate destination details carefully
- Review minimum receive conditions
- Prefer providers with stable completion quality

## FAQ

### Is swapping ADA on a DEX always cheaper?
Not always. It depends on liquidity, timing, and destination-chain costs.

### Can I avoid slippage completely?
No, but you can reduce it by checking timing, protection settings, and route quality.

### Is ADA swap execution instant?
Execution time varies by route and network conditions.
