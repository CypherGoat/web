Users often ask: should I swap directly on a large exchange like Binance, or use an aggregator?

The right answer depends on your constraints.

## Binance-style direct route: strengths

- familiar UX for many users
- deep liquidity in major pairs
- broad product ecosystem

## Binance-style direct route: limits

- route optionality is platform-bounded
- policy/compliance changes can affect flow suddenly
- may not be best execution for niche corridors

## Aggregator route: strengths

- multi-provider comparison in one place
- better chance of finding edge in specific corridors
- flexible routing when one provider degrades

## Aggregator route: limits

- quality depends on provider set and monitoring
- users must understand route-level differences

## Decision framework

Use direct exchange route when:
- your pair is highly liquid there,
- you need that specific venue workflow,
- and execution quality is consistently good.

Use aggregator route when:
- you want best-route discovery,
- you care about provider optionality,
- or you’re swapping across variable-liquidity corridors.

## Risk checklist before choosing

- Is route quality measured by completed output, not just quote?
- Is there fallback when a provider underperforms?
- Are refund and exception paths clear?
- Are you comfortable with compliance friction profile?

## Practical recommendation

For most users, the best process is:
1. compare route options first,
2. check reliability context,
3. execute where expected net output is strongest.
